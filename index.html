Contract Essentials

Legal Framework

Understand the Queensland Domestic Building Contracts Act 2000, which mandates written contracts for domestic building work over $3,300, including clear terms on scope, price, and completion dates. Ensure compliance with QBCC licensing and insurance requirements.

Fair Agreements

Contracts should include balanced terms, such as a maximum 5% deposit, clear payment schedules tied to milestones, and provisions for variations agreed upon in writing by both parties to ensure fairness and transparency.

Dispute Resolution

Include clauses for mediation through the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT) or QBCC dispute resolution processes. Specify timelines for raising issues and steps for escalation to avoid costly litigation.

Scope of Work

Detail all work to be performed, including materials, specifications, and exclusions. Attach plans and specifications to the contract to prevent disputes over expectations and ensure clarity on project deliverables.

Frequently Asked Questions

Apartment & Unit Contracts

Yes, standard domestic building contracts apply to apartments and units, but with some additional considerations. Multi-unit developments often have:

  • Strata title implications that affect contract terms
  • Shared infrastructure requirements
  • Additional compliance with body corporate rules
  • Different insurance requirements compared to standalone homes

Always ensure your contract specifically addresses these multi-unit considerations as of May 1, 2025.

Defect liability in apartments follows the same statutory warranty periods as standalone homes (typically 6 years for structural defects), but with additional complexity:

  • The builder must coordinate with the body corporate for common area repairs
  • Defects affecting multiple units may require a coordinated repair approach
  • Access arrangements for repairs must consider privacy of other residents
  • Your contract should specify how defect notifications will be handled in a multi-unit context

Updated as of May 1, 2025.

Builder Liquidation Protections

If your builder enters liquidation:

  • The contract is typically terminated automatically
  • You'll need to engage a new builder to complete the work
  • Any unspent deposit money should be recoverable through insurance
  • Progress payments for incomplete work may be claimable
  • Statutory warranties may still apply to completed work

Your first step should be contacting your state's building authority and consulting a construction lawyer as of May 1, 2025.
